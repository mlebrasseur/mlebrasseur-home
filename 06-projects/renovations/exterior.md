## Scope

- Combined garage driveway/drainage/retaining-wall project:
  - redo the garage driveway;
  - correct garage entrance grading;
  - review and correct the exterior garage drain basin and water infiltration risk;
  - account for garage sump/drain context;
  - address right-side driveway retaining-wall settlement;
  - choose material and slope design before final quote acceptance.
- Foundation crack project:
  - get specialist review and quotes for the reported foundation cracks, including the front-left crack;
  - determine whether the front-left crack should be repaired from the interior or exterior;
  - if exterior excavation is recommended, compare a turnkey specialist quote with a separately priced excavation/backfill option.

## Work-zone clarification

- The visible foundation crack is at the front-left of the house.
- The garage driveway is at the front-right of the house; there is no visible foundation crack at the driveway.
- These are separate work zones. The crack repair does not need to be completed before the driveway, and the two projects may proceed independently or in parallel.
- If exterior access is required for the front-left crack, there may still be savings from coordinating machinery, mobilization, soil removal, backfill, or site restoration. Treat this as an optional pricing opportunity, not a technical dependency.
- Keep the foundation specialist responsible for the repair method, waterproofing specification, inspection before backfill, and repair warranty. Any shared excavation scope must state clearly who is responsible for excavation, backfill, settlement, restoration, and damage.

## Quote request checklist

### Driveway quote

- Price the garage driveway as its own front-right project; do not imply that it contains or covers a foundation crack.
- Include removal, excavation and disposal; granular base depth and material; geotextile if proposed; compaction method; asphalt or paver specification; finished drainage slopes; garage threshold transition; catch-basin/drain work; right-side retaining-wall correction; reinstatement; exclusions; taxes; warranty; and September 2026 availability.
- Ask the contractor to identify drainage or base conditions that could change the price after excavation.
- As an optional add-on only, ask whether the contractor could price excavation, backfill and restoration at the front-left foundation if a foundation specialist provides the scope and supervises the repair before backfill.

### Foundation quote

- Identify the crack location as front-left and ask the specialist to inspect the other reported cracks as part of the visit.
- Request the diagnosis, recommended interior or exterior repair method, crack preparation/injection, waterproofing or membrane work, excavation limits, backfill and compaction, surface restoration, exclusions, taxes, warranty, and September 2026 availability.
- If exterior work is recommended, request two prices where practical: a complete turnkey price and a repair/waterproofing-only price if excavation and backfill are coordinated separately.
- Require one contractor to retain clear responsibility for the repair system and warranty. Do not split the work unless both written scopes define the handoff and responsibilities.
